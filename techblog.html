INTERVIEW WITH AN OLD PERSON

by, Mekayla Young

Akan Metzger is a director/cinematographer located in New York city. He currently works in a school as the head of film studies, sharing his knowledge of film with students.

Student:
How old were you when you got your first computer?

Alan:
50 years old.

Student:
What was your favorite piece of technology growing up?

Alan:
Television.

Student:
How has tecnology changed over your lifetime?

Alan:
We got a TV when I was 4 years old. The TVs in my house had no dial, you would pick up and an operator would come up. You also had to share phone lines with your neighbors. There was always a fight to use the phone and TVs with color didn't come until 10 years later. TV would go off air at 10pm and come back on at 6am. There were no computers or cellphones.

Student:
Have the changes you've seen in technology over your lifetime mostly helped or hurt society?

Alan:
It's a mixed bag. On one having a phone makes you feel safer being alone. On the other hand it allows ppl to do a mediocre job. You can use photoshop and be okay, but does that make you a visual artist? it makes a bad job okay.

Student:
What is your greatest hope for technology in the future?

Alan:
Driver-less cars...I think the email thing is phenomenal, it makes business life easier.

Student:
What is your greatest fear for technology in the future?

Alan:
Tech increasingly has a mind of its own, that concerns me. Fake news is increasing...Anyone can become a news reporter and submit a news letter. It makes everything less reliable.

Student:
What advice would you like to share with the younger generation about technology?

Alan:
Don't be too reliable on [technology]. They're machines... machines always fail, so don't be too reliable on it.

Student:
If you create any form of tech, what would it be?

Alan:
A source of compeletely clean energy.
